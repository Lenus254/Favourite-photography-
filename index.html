The big five Biography

The “Big Five” is a term that is used to refer to the 5 African animals that early big game hunters considered most difficult and dangerous animals to hunt on foot in Africa.

These animals include the Elephant, Lion, Leopard, Buffalo, and Rhinoceros.

Here are the names of the big five

  1. Lion
  2. Elephant
  3. Buffalo
  4. Leopard
  5. Rhino

Photographs and brief descriptions

Elephant

elephant, (family Elephantidae), largest living land animal, characterized by its long trunk (elongated upper lip and nose), columnar legs

2. Lions

Lions have strong, compact bodies and powerful forelegs, teeth and jaws for pulling down and killing prey.

3. Rhinoceros

Rhinos are thought to be the second largest land animal, with the elephant being the largest

4. Bufallo

These are large hoofed mammals with an imposing pair of horns atop their heads. Both males and females have horns.

4. Leopard

Leopards are large cats, with light-colored fur, and black spots and rosettes across their entire body.
